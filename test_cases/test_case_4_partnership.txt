PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

This Partnership Agreement ("Agreement") is made effective as of April 1, 2025, among the following partners ("Partners"):

PARTNER A: Jessica Williams, residing at 789 Elm Street, Chicago, IL 60614
PARTNER B: Michael Chen, residing at 321 Oak Avenue, Chicago, IL 60610
PARTNER C: Aisha Patel, residing at 456 Maple Drive, Chicago, IL 60611

PARTNERSHIP NAME: Innovative Consulting Partners LLC

1. FORMATION AND PURPOSE
The Partners hereby form a limited liability partnership for the purpose of providing business consulting, strategic planning, and management advisory services to corporate clients.

2. PRINCIPAL PLACE OF BUSINESS
The partnership's principal office shall be located at 1200 West Loop, Suite 400, Chicago, IL 60607.

3. TERM
This Partnership shall commence on May 1, 2025, and shall continue until dissolved by mutual agreement of all Partners or as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

4. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS
4.1 Initial Contributions:
- Partner A (Jessica Williams): $100,000.00 cash (40% ownership)
- Partner B (Michael Chen): $75,000.00 cash (30% ownership)
- Partner C (Aisha Patel): $75,000.00 cash (30% ownership)
Total Initial Capital: $250,000.00

4.2 Additional capital contributions may be required by unanimous vote of all Partners.
4.3 Failure to make required contributions may result in dilution of ownership percentage.

5. PROFIT AND LOSS ALLOCATION
Profits and losses shall be allocated according to ownership percentages:
- Partner A: 40%
- Partner B: 30%
- Partner C: 30%

Distributions shall be made quarterly within 15 days after the end of each calendar quarter.

6. MANAGEMENT AND VOTING
6.1 All major decisions require unanimous approval, including:
   - Admission of new partners
   - Sale or merger of the partnership
   - Borrowing exceeding $50,000.00
   - Changes to partnership agreement
   - Dissolution of partnership

6.2 Day-to-day operational decisions require approval of Partners holding 51% or more of ownership interests.

6.3 Each Partner shall have voting rights proportional to their ownership percentage.

7. DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
7.1 Partner A (Jessica Williams): Managing Partner - Overall business strategy, client relationships, and operations
7.2 Partner B (Michael Chen): Finance Partner - Financial management, accounting, and reporting
7.3 Partner C (Aisha Patel): Operations Partner - Project management, team coordination, and service delivery

7.4 Each Partner agrees to devote their full business time and attention to partnership affairs.
7.5 Partners may not engage in any competing business without unanimous consent.

8. COMPENSATION
8.1 Base Compensation:
   - Partner A: $120,000.00 annually
   - Partner B: $100,000.00 annually
   - Partner C: $100,000.00 annually

8.2 Performance Bonuses: Additional distributions based on partnership performance and individual contributions, determined annually.

9. EXPENSES
All reasonable business expenses shall be reimbursed upon submission of proper documentation within 30 days.

10. BOOKS AND RECORDS
The partnership shall maintain complete books and records at the principal office. Each Partner shall have access to all partnership records during normal business hours. Annual financial statements shall be prepared by a certified public accountant.

11. BANKING
All partnership funds shall be deposited in accounts established in the partnership name. Checks exceeding $25,000.00 require signatures of two Partners.

12. ADMISSION OF NEW PARTNERS
New partners may be admitted only by unanimous consent of existing Partners. Terms of admission, including capital contribution and ownership percentage, shall be determined by the existing Partners.

13. WITHDRAWAL OF PARTNERS
13.1 A Partner may withdraw by providing 90 days' written notice to other Partners.
13.2 The withdrawing Partner's interest shall be valued at fair market value as determined by an independent business appraiser.
13.3 Payment shall be made in 24 equal monthly installments beginning 30 days after withdrawal.

14. DEATH OR DISABILITY
14.1 Upon death of a Partner, their ownership interest shall be purchased by the remaining Partners at fair market value.
14.2 Each Partner shall maintain life insurance of at least $500,000.00 with the partnership as beneficiary.
14.3 Permanent disability (inability to work for 180 consecutive days) shall trigger buyout provisions similar to death.

15. DISSOLUTION
15.1 The Partnership may be dissolved by:
   (a) Unanimous written agreement of all Partners
   (b) Death or withdrawal of a Partner if remaining Partners do not agree to continue
   (c) Bankruptcy of the Partnership
   (d) Court order

15.2 Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed in the following order:
   (a) Payment of debts and liabilities
   (b) Return of capital contributions
   (c) Distribution of remaining assets according to ownership percentages

16. NON-COMPETE
Upon withdrawal or termination, a Partner shall not compete within a 50-mile radius of Chicago for a period of two (2) years or solicit partnership clients or employees.

17. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Disputes shall be resolved through mediation. If unresolved after 30 days, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration in Chicago, Illinois.

18. GOVERNING LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Illinois.
